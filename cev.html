Cornell Electric Vehicles (CEV) is a fully student run project team dedicated to building autonomous, hyper-efficient electric cars. Our deeply knowledgeable interdisciplinary organization operates at the pace of a startup and has repeatedly produced industry-grade vehicles. Having placed highly at Shell Eco-Marathon Americas, we are a respected team operating at the limits of emergent technologies. Endeavoring to integrate level 2 autonomy into our vehicles reflects our determination to continue forging at the leading edge of innovation. Our team's website is https://www.cornellelectricvehicles.org/.

Chassis Lead

Chassis Surface Model for UC24

As Chassis Lead I oversaw the manufacturing of the Monocoque and Aerodyanic Chassis Components for the UC24 vehicle, the Aerodyanamic and Structural Design of the UC25 Chassis, and the integration process of all mechanical, electrical and autonomy hardware systems. Our UC24 Model was designed in Autodesk Inventor using Surfacing tools, but I lead the switch to Autodesk Alias (A top Automotive Surface Modeling Program) for the design of the UC25 Vehicle. Through an iteritive design process, alterning model parameters and analysing aerodynamic performance using Ansys Fluent Computational Fluid Mechanics, I was able to reduce drag by 6% while also altering many parts for easier manufacturablity with specific surfaces for Autonomous Sensor Mounting. We then analyse and optimize our structural components using Ansys ACP and Mechanical.
